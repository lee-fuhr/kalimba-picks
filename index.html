How we choose our picks

We don’t run lab tests or timed trials, instead, we rely on a combination of long‑term owner feedback from forums, video reviews by experienced players, and published write‑ups from trusted music publications. We look for patterns in what actually breaks: weak tines, poor tuning stability, sharp edges, or dead spots in the upper register. A model that consistently earns praise after months of use earns our recommendation. We also consider real‑world factors like how easy it is to replace tines, whether the thumb holes are comfortably sized, and how the finish holds up to humidity. Our picks aren’t the flashiest or most expensive, they’re the ones that most people will be happiest playing a year after purchase.
